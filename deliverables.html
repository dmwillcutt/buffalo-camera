Deliverables and Technology

The project will facilitate remote participation with the bison herd by online visitors through a combination of cutting edge surveillence technology, community building digital architecture, and guidance from park staff and indigenous leaders.

Experience Wanuskewin Heritage Park's bison herd from a new perspective

See the majesty of nature up close and personal and learn about the intertwined efforts of bison preservation, the promotion of indigenous cultures, and stewardship of the land through the cold, uncompromising lens of technology.

Communications. Unity. Community.

The project will enable remote observation of the bison herd by online visitors. This will also allow previous visitors to the Heritage Park to keep track of the bison they have interacted with. Using this app, users can follow the trail of the bison groups either on-site at the heritage park or remotely worldwide. Interaction is available through a web based interface or app.

Cutting Edge Surveillance

The project will uses stationary cameras and an autonomous drone swarm equipped with revolutionary HD imaging optics, including night vision and "Predator" infrared. As a optional measure of security, drones can be armed with less than lethal short range crowd control devices including electroshock weaponry and belt-fed rubber bullet miniguns.
