Wanuskewin Buffalo Herd

Deliverables/Outcomes: The project will enable remote participation with the bison herd by online visitors. This will also allow previous visitors to the Heritage Park to keep track of the bison they have interacted with. This will generate interest as well as drive potential monetary contributions to the Park and sale of bison related merchandise (stuffed animals, naming rights for bison calves, etc…). Using this app, users can follow the trace of the bison groups either on-site at the heritage park or remotely worldwide. The app is available through either a mobile device or desktop. Functionality & Technology The core of the project will be an app available via mobile devices or computers. The bison will be outfitted with GPS receivers that will allow their movement through the park grounds to be tracked and mapped in real time. Linked to this will be a series of cameras to allow visitors the ability to watch the bison at different points throughout the Heritage Park. Visitors will use the app to view the movements of the bison. They can select certain bison to follow and the app will give a specific overview of that bison’s actions along with video and photos. The app will let visitors know where the bison are located and switch to the appropriate camera. Footage of the bison will be recorded and selected moments available for playback to track the life of the bison herd, especially with calves and young bison. Visitors can screenshot images they find of interest and share with other visitors. This function can be connected to visitors social media platforms. The Park Twitter account will retweet the more interesting images and comments. Visitors can ask questions through this function and Park staff can reply. Throughout the app there will be opportunities for visitors to click on informative articles that discuss the importance of the bison to the Indigenous people of the North Plains. To take maximum advantage of the digital platform, segments will incorporate the Park interpreters and rely on visual storytelling. The app will present these video segments and links to information on exhibits that relate to the story of the bison and their relationship to the land and the Indigenous people of Canada. Using video will allow updates on new exhibits and discussion, allowing the app to be constantly updated. Cutting Edge Surveillance Technology

The bison will be observed by a combination of stationary cameras and an autonomous drone swarm using high definition video, revolutionary night vision lenses and thermal sensing optics. Due to breakthroughs in proprietary battery technology, the drone swarm will be able to stay aloft for weeks at a time. As a optional measure of security against poachers, Class III drones can be armed with non-lethal short range crowd control and deterrent devices including electroshock weaponry, electric nets, and belt-fed rubber bullet miniguns.
